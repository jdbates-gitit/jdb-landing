A Framework That Changed Everything

The world doesn't happen
to you. It happens for you.

During COVID, when the world went home and the walls started closing in, I was managing the pipeline for a high-producing residential mortgage branch. The stress followed me everywhere — into the evenings, into my weekends, into my sleep. I couldn't separate from it.

My branch manager — my boss at the time — voluntold me to work with a life and professional coach named Steve Reiner from Southwestern Consulting. I didn't ask for it. I didn't know I needed it. The universe did.

Steve introduced me to the Belief Cycle — something I had always known about on some level, but had never truly sat with. When he walked me through it, something shifted in my foundations. I began to see, for the first time with real clarity, that my beliefs and attitudes were dictating the world I was living in.

The world wasn't happening to me. It was happening for me — every second of every day. My difficulties and my gifts, my setbacks and my breakthroughs — all of it was part of the experience. All of it was enriching me. The moment I could reframe what I believed about any situation — in a way that was honest, healthy, and good — I had direct control over my results.

I still feel fear every day. I still sit in moments where I wonder if I belong on a call with the president of a company, whether I've earned my seat at the table. The Belief Cycle taught me to stop inside that fear and recognize two things: first, that I'm feeling it because I'm growing — and growth is supposed to feel like this. Second, that I am meant to be there. I have earned it. It is not an accident.

Today it lives in my toolbox. When I notice the results I'm producing aren't what I want, I trace them back — through my actions, my emotions, my thoughts — all the way to the belief underneath. Change the belief, and you change the trajectory. I've watched it happen in my own life more times than I can count.

The Framework
Five steps. One cycle. Everything connected.

"Change what you believe, and you change everything that follows — your thoughts, your feelings, your actions, and ultimately your life."

A paraphrase of The Belief Cycle · Southwestern Consulting

The Five Steps
Step 01
Beliefs
The root of everything. What you accept as true about yourself, others, and the world shapes every thought you'll have today. This is where the work starts — and where the most powerful changes happen.
Step 02
Thoughts
Beliefs generate a constant internal dialogue. Your mind looks for evidence of what it already believes — and finds it everywhere. What you dwell on expands. You get to choose what you dwell on.
Step 03
Emotions
Thoughts activate feelings. Fear, confidence, doubt, energy — these aren't random. They're the emotional signature of what you're thinking right now. Trace the feeling back far enough and you'll find a belief.
Step 04
Actions
How you feel determines what you do, what you avoid, and what you endure. Emotions create momentum or friction. The action is downstream of the belief — which means changing the belief changes the behavior.
Step 05
Results
Your results are the visible, external evidence of what you believe on the inside. When something isn't working, trace it back. The answer is almost never in the result itself — it's upstream, in the belief that started the cycle.

The world doesn't happen to you. Every difficulty, every gift, every moment of fear and every breakthrough — it's all happening for you. That's not wishful thinking. That's a decision about what to believe.
